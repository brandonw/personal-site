    BRANDON WASKIEWICZ

    I am a software developer with interests varying from new programming language development and theory, to game development, to web development. I love languages with great type systems (current favorites being Rust and Haskell), as well as languages whose open source communities contribute prodigious amounts of awesome code (like Python). I also play with C every once in a while to keep me honest, and use C# when I am working on Windows-centric software.

    My GitHub account can be found here. I can be reached via e-mail at brandon.waskiewicz@gmail.com.

    ABOUT ME

    I grew up with an interest in experimenting with computers. I have fond memories of connecting to Bulletin Board Systems as a kid, using up my daily playtime on L.O.R.D., and then starting up QBasic and trying to create my own version-- ASCII graphics start screen and all.

    That eventually led to me trying to learn C++ by reading Borland's Turbo C++ manual during lunch periods in middle school (even though it was admittedly over my head at the time), and culminating in my choice of Computer Science major at the Univeristy of Massachusetts at Amherst.
